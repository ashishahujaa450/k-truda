Keytruda® in combination with pemetrexed and platinum chemotherapy

New indication in combination with pemetrexed and platinum chemotherapy in previously untreated patients with metastatic non-squamous non-small cell lung cancer (mNSCLC)

PrKEYTRUDA® (pembrolizumab) in combination with pemetrexed and platinum chemotherapy, is indicated for the treatment of metastatic non-squamous NSCLC, in adults with no EGFR or ALK genomic tumour aberrations, and no prior systemic chemotherapy treatment for metastatic NSCLC.1

KEYTRUDA® + pemetrexed and investigator’s choice of platinum chemotherapy (plat/pem) significantly reduced the risk of death compared with placebo + plat/pem1

Study Design

KEYNOTE-189 is the pivotal phase 3, multicentre, randomized, double-blind trial that evaluated efficacy and safety profile of KEYTRUDA® in combination with pemetrexed and investigator’s choice of platinum chemotherapy vs. placebo + pemetrexed and investigator’s choice of platinum chemotherapy in patients with non-squamous mNSCLC.1

1
Inclusion Criteria
  • Metastatic non-squamous NSCLC
  • No prior systemic treatment for mNSCLC
  • No EGFR or ALK genomic tumour aberrations1
2
Study Arms

KEYTRUDA®

KEYTRUDA® 200 mg + pemetrexed 500 mg/m2 + investigator’s choice of cisplatin 75 mg/m2 or carboplatin area under the curve (AUC) 5 mg/mL/min IV every 3 weeks for 4 cycles followed by KEYTRUDA® 200 mg + pemetrexed 500 mg/m2 IV every 3 weeks. KEYTRUDA® was administered prior to chemotherapy (n=410).1

PLACEBO

Placebo + pemetrexed 500 mg/m2 + investigator’s choice of cisplatin 75 mg/m2 or carboplatin AUC 5 mg/mL/min IV every 3 weeks for 4 cycles followed by placebo + pemetrexed 500 mg/m2 IV every 3 weeks (n=206).1

Discontinuations

Treatment was discontinued for treatment-related AEs in: 9.6% of the 405 patients receiving KEYTRUDA® + plat/pem and in 4.0% of the 202 patients receiving placebo + plat/pem.1

The most common treatment-related AEs leading to study drug discontinuation (occurring in > 3 patients) were acute kidney injury (n=7) and pneumonitis (n=7).1

The median time to discontinuation for treatment-related AEs was 4.0 months.1

Safety information

Keytruda®

Safety Information

Indications and clinical use:

PrKEYTRUDA® (pembrolizumab) has been issued marketing authorization with conditions, pending the results of studies to verify its clinical benefit. Patients should be advised of the nature of the authorization.

  • KEYTRUDA® is indicated for the treatment of patients with metastatic non-small cell lung carcinoma (NSCLC) as monotherapy, in adults whose tumours express PD-L1 [(Tumour Proportion Score (TPS) ≥1%)] as determined by a validated test and who have disease progression on or after platinum-containing chemotherapy. Patients with EGFR or ALK genomic tumour aberrations should have received authorized therapy for these aberrations prior to receiving KEYTRUDA®

    • KEYTRUDA® has been issued marketing authorization without conditions for the treatment of patients with:

  • Metastatic non-squamous NSCLC in combination with pemetrexed and platinum chemotherapy, in adults with no EGFR or ALK genomic tumour aberrations, and no prior systemic chemotherapy treatment for metastatic NSCLC.
  • Metastatic non-small cell lung carcinoma (NSCLC) as monotherapy, in adults whose tumours have high PD-L1 expression [(Tumour Proportion Score (TPS) ≥50%)] as determined by a validated test, with no EGFR or ALK genomic tumour aberrations, and no prior systemic chemotherapy treatment for metastatic NSCLC.

    • The safety and efficacy of KEYTRUDA® has not been established for pediatric patients with conditions other than relapsed or refractory PMBCL.

    Relevant warnings and precautions:

  • Immune-mediated adverse reactions including severe and fatal cases:
    • Pneumonitis
    • Colitis
    • Hepatitis
    • Nephritis and renal dysfunction
    • Endocrinopathies including hypophysitis, type 1 diabetes mellitus and thyroid disorders
    • Severe skin reactions, including Stevens-Johnson syndrome and toxic epidermal necrolysis
  • Other immune-mediated adverse events including uveitis, arthritis, myositis, encephalitis, sarcoidosis, myasthenic syndrome, vasculitis, Guillain-Barré syndrome, hemolytic anemia and pancreatitis
  • Myocarditis
  • Solid organ transplant rejection
  • Increased mortality in patients with multiple myeloma when added to a thalidomide analogue and dexamethasone
  • Complications of allogeneic Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT) in classical Hodgkin Lymphoma
  • Severe infusion-related reactions, including hypersensitivity and anaphylaxis
  • Embryofetal toxicity
  • Not recommended in pregnant women
  • In nursing women, a decision should be made whether to discontinue breast-feeding or KEYTRUDA® taking into account the benefit of breast-feeding for the child and the benefit of KEYTRUDA® therapy for the woman
  • Has not been studied in patients with moderate or severe hepatic impairment
  • Has not been studied in patients with severe renal impairment
  • Monitor liver and thyroid function tests and electrolytes during treatment
